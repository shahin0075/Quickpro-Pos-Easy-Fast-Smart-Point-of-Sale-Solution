Dashboard

The QuickPro POS Dashboard provides a centralized view of your business's financial and operational metrics. Here's an explanation of its key components:

  1. Date Range Selector:

    • Allows you to select a specific date or range of dates for filtering data.
    • Default selection is "Today," but it can be customized for any period.

  2. Summary Metrics :

    • Total Receivable: Displays the total amount receivable from customers, along with the number of parties involved.
    • Total Payable: Shows the total amount payable to suppliers or other entities.
    • Total Sale: Highlights the overall sales for the selected period.
    • Purchase: Tracks the amount spent on purchases within the date range.
    • Cash in Hand: Reflects the current cash balance available.
    • Bank Balance: Displays the total amount in linked bank accounts.

  3. Sales Graph:

    • A visual representation of sales trends over time.
    • Provides insights into monthly or daily sales performance.

POS

Key Features:

  1. Header Section:

    • Displays "QuickproPos" in the title bar.
    • Shows the location dropdown (Main Warehouse) and current date/time.
    • Includes a dashboard navigation button at the top-right corner.

  2. Search and Filters:

    • A customer dropdown labeled "Walk In Customer."
    • A search bar with the placeholder "Scan/Search product by name/barcode."
    • Category and brand filters on the right.

  3. Product List (Cart Table):

    • A table displays the following columns: Item, Qty, Rate, Discount(%), Total, and Actions.
    • The quantity column has buttons to increase (+) or decrease (-) the count for each product.
    • A trash icon allows items to be removed.
    • The "Total" column calculates the line item total.

  4. Scrollable Right Panel:

    • Contains products with images, names, and likely the ability to add them to the cart.

  5. Summary Section (Bottom):

    • Displays quick summaries:
      • Quantity, Taxable Amount, Tax Amount, Discount, Flat Discount, and Total Amount.

  6. Action Buttons:

    • Bottom-left:
      • "Hold Bills" and "On Hold Bills."
    • Bottom-right:
      • "Clear Cart [F2]."
      • Payment options: Cash [F1], Card [F2], UPI [F3].
      • "Register Details" and "Recent Transaction."

Products

Key Features

  • Product Type: Dropdown menu to select the type (default: "Product").
  • Product Name: Textbox to enter the name of the product.
  • Item / Barcode: A pre-filled field showing a generated barcode.
  • HSN Code: Textbox for entering the HSN (Harmonized System Nomenclature) code.
  • Category: Dropdown menu for selecting the product category.
  • Brand: Dropdown menu for selecting the product brand.
  • Unit: Dropdown menu for selecting the unit of measurement.
  • Warehouse: Dropdown menu for selecting the warehouse (default: "Main Warehouse").
  • Tax: Dropdown menu for selecting the applicable tax rate.
  • Purchase Rate: Numeric field with an option to include/exclude tax.
  • MRP (Maximum Retail Price): Numeric field for entering the MRP.
  • Sales Rate: Numeric field with an option to include/exclude tax.
  • Opening Qty: Numeric field for entering the initial quantity.
  • Opening Date: Date picker to specify the opening date (default: current date).
  • Description: Text area for adding a product description.
  • Product Image: File input to upload an image of the product.

Sales Invoice

Features

Form Fields

  1. Invoice No

    • Displays a unique identifier for the invoice, typically auto-generated.

  2. Customer Name

    • Select the customer for the invoice. Mandatory field with an option to create a new customer if not already in the system.

  3. Voucher Date

    • Specify the date of the invoice. Mandatory field.

  4. Due Date

    • Optional field to indicate when payment is due.

  5. Warehouse

    • Select the warehouse where goods are stored. Mandatory field.

  6. Reference No

    • An optional field for additional references, such as purchase order numbers.

Product Details Table

This section captures the details of products included in the invoice.

  1. Product

    • Select a product from the list of available products.

  2. Qty (Quantity)

    • Enter the quantity of the selected product.

  3. Unit

    • Displays the unit of measurement for the product.

  4. Rate

    • Set the price per unit of the product.

  5. Discount (%)

    • Apply a percentage discount to the product.

  6. Tax

    • Select the applicable tax for the product.

  7. Total

    • Displays the calculated total for the row (Qty × Rate - Discount + Tax).

  8. Action

    • Allows deletion of a product row.

Notes and Summary Section

  1. Notes

    • Add optional remarks or additional information about the invoice.

  2. Summary Fields

    • Sub Total: Displays the sum of all product totals before any discounts or taxes.
    • Flat Discount (%): Apply a percentage discount to the entire invoice.
    • Taxable Total: Displays the total amount after applying the discount but before adding taxes.
    • Tax Amount: Displays the total tax applied to the invoice.
    • Round Off: Rounds the total amount to the nearest whole number.
    • Grand Total: Displays the final payable amount, including all adjustments.
    • Received: Enter the amount received from the customer.

Action Buttons

  1. Cancel

    • Redirects the user back to the sales listing page without saving.

  2. Save

    • Saves the invoice. Includes a spinner to indicate processing if the operation takes time.

Payment In

Payment Details Section

  • Receipt No: Shows a unique identifier for the payment receipt (auto-generated and read-only).
  • Customer: Dropdown for selecting the customer making the payment. Changing this updates customer-specific data.
  • Receipt From: Dropdown to select a cash or bank account where the payment is deposited.
  • Date: Input field for selecting the payment date with a calendar icon.
  • Amount: Displays the payment total and flags it if the amount is zero (indicating all dues are settled).
  • Notes: Input field for adding optional comments or details about the payment.

Receipt Allocations Section

  • Columns:

    1. Bill No: Lists invoice or bill numbers.
    2. Date: Displays the corresponding dates of the invoices.
    3. Amount: Shows the total amount for each bill.
    4. Left to Allocate: Indicates the unpaid balance for the bill.
    5. This Allocation: Input field to specify the payment amount allocated to a bill.
    6. Action: Provides the ability to delete allocations for a specific bill.

Action Buttons

  • Cancel: Redirects the user to another page without saving changes.
  • Save: Finalizes the payment record and its allocations. The button is disabled during processing or when necessary fields are incomplete.

Payment Out

Payment Details Section:

  • Receipt No: A field displaying the receipt number, which is readonly.
  • Supplier Selection: A dropdown for selecting a supplier, where the user is required to choose one.
  • Paid From: A dropdown to choose from different cash or bank accounts from which the payment is made. This is also a mandatory field.
  • Date: A date input field for the transaction date, with a calendar picker.
  • Amount: Displays the payment amount as a readonly field. If the amount is zero, it shows a message indicating that the payment has already been made.
  • Notes: A text input to add any additional notes about the payment.

3. Payment Allocations Section:

  • Table of Allocations:
    • A table is displayed with columns for Bill No, Date, Amount, Left to Allocate, and This Allocation.
    • there is an option to allocate a portion of the payment. The user can enter the amount they want to allocate in the This Allocation field, and the page will adjust based on the entered value.
    • There’s also a Delete button for removing an allocation.

4. Action Buttons:

  • Cancel Button: A button that cancels the operation and navigates back
  • Save Button: A button that saves the payment details. It’s disabled while the transaction is being processed (isProcessing). If it’s processing, a loading spinner is shown.

Features:

  • Conditional Rendering:
    • The "Save" button is only enabled if the payment amount is greater than zero.
    • There’s also a "Already paid" message that appears when the amount is zero.

Summary:

This page facilitates entering payment details for payments made to suppliers, allowing users to allocate amounts to bills, choose the cash/bank account used for the payment, and save the transaction. The user can also cancel the operation and navigate back to the previous page.
